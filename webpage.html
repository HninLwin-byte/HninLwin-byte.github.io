Data Augmentation of Alaryngeal Speech Using Voice Conversion with Timbre Enhanced Loss

Abstract

The primary goal of voice conversion is to transform the vocal characteristics of speech while preserving its linguistic content. In this paper, we introduce a data augmentation technique for alaryngeal speech using voice conversion within the MaskCycleGAN-VC framework. Our method leverages two innovative masking techniques: Articulatory Dimension Masking (ADM) and the combination of ADM with Consecutive Time Masking (CTM), referred to as Specmix. The original method employed for masking in MaskCycleGAN-VC seems to be CTM, and our novel masking techniques enhance the quality and performance of voice conversion for alaryngeal speech. We can also expand the variability of voice characteristics within the converted alaryngeal speech dataset. One notable enhancement in our approach is the incorporation of a timbre similarity score into the generator loss, known as the Timbre Enhanced Loss. This score dynamically guides the conversion process to prioritize the preservation of timbral characteristics during voice transformation.

Training Settings

As part of the preprocessing stage, Mel-spectrograms underwent normalization using training dataset statistics. Employing a least-squares GAN as our GAN objective, the training process spanned 5k iterations, with an Adam optimizer employed. Specifically, the converter and discriminator utilized learning rates of 0.0002 and 0.0001, respectively. Momentum terms were established as β1 = 0.5 and β2 = 0.999. The batch size was defined as 1, where individual training instances encompassed 64 randomly cropped frames, approximately equating to a duration of 0.75 seconds. Hyperparameters, λcyc, λid, and λresem were configured at values of 10, 5, and 0.0001.
